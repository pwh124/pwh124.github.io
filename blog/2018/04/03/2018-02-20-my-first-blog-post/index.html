My first blog post!

By Paul Hook | April 3, 2018

Hello! If you haven’t been able to tell by the website already, my name is Paul Hook.

I am a fourth year Ph.D. student in the Human Genetics program in the McKusick-Nathans Institute of Genetic Medicine at the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine. I am in Dr. Andy McCallion’s lab.

You can learn a little more about me here, so I won’t go on and on in the inaugural blog post.

One of the reasons I was driven to create a website/blog was the blog of one of my former Human Genetics program-mates, Shannon Ellis. I truly enjoy her blog and I have seen the ways in which it has allowed her to have voice in the sometimes noisy, scientific discussion. Please, please, please, check out her blog/website here: https://shanellis.github.io/about/ and her Twitter.
Another reason (which grew out of my admiration of Shannon’s blog) was the community of R users/data scientists that regularly share their work and thoughts through their websites. I have recently learned that I enjoy data analysis and presentation (especially spending too much time tinkering with graphs) and I have found myself constantly thinking of fun data analyses to do. I am hoping that I can share some interesting ideas I think I have.

Finally, as a Ph.D. student in the age of social media…I wanted to promote myself and my work. I thought a website would help me do that. We will see.

I just want to shout out two big contributors that helped me wade through the deep end of making a website using Hugo and R:

  • The R package blogdown. This was a great resource that had simple directions for set up and deployment of my site. The Github for blogdown can be found here: https://github.com/rstudio/blogdown

  • DevCows for making the Hugo universal theme that I used as the base to my website. The theme can be found here: https://github.com/devcows/hugo-universal-theme

So…please poke around as much as you want and I hope to have more posts about R/food/science soon!

In honor of my first blog post and hopefully many more to come, I will include a simple R graph, a normal distribution!
